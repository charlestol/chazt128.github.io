SeekIt


Team Size: 4

Role: Lead Programmer

Tech Utilized: ReactJS (web), React Native (iOS and Android), Firebase, Bootstrap

Description: This was a semester-long project for the Software Engineering course (CSE 120) at UC Merced. Our team, along with 2 separate teams had CZI (Chan Zuckerberg Initiative) as an industry sponsor and they instructed us to create an educational scavenger hunt application. We decided on developing this app to be on both web and mobile platforms mainly because we wanted to provide more options to different preferences, such as instructors preferring to use the web app for creating events and grading submissions. Our team was successful in developing an MVP (Minimum Viable Product) of both the web and mobile app within 3 months. Our app won 1st place in the CZI industry category (we competed with the 2 other CZI teams), which allowed our team to participate as a finalist at UC Merced's annuel Mobile App Challenge (competed against other industries).

LilBill

Team Size: 3

Role: Programmer

Tech Utilized: React Native (iOS), TuriCreate

Description: This app was developed to be used for a user case study, recording the effectiveness of audio (speech) and tactile (vibrations) in different environments, for the Human-Computer Interaction course (CSE 155) at UC Merced. Our app is rather simple, it has the camera on the whole time and while a dollar bill is not in the camera view, it will display "NOT DOLLAR BILL". Otherwise, if there is a bill in the view, it is able to identify that bill's USD value. This is possible due to the 20,000+ images (online and personally taken) that we processed with TuriCreate to create a custom machine learning model. Although our team didn't have to develop an actual app for this type of study, we thought it would be worthwhile to build something for fun (took about 2 months). If you've ever heard of the show "Silicon Valley", our app was inspired by their "Not Hotdog" app which tells the user whether or not they are looking at a hotdog.
